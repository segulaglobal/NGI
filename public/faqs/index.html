Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

NGI envisions a positive impact on communities by empowering girls and women to become agents of change, leading to improved well-being and socio-economic development.

NGI promotes global solidarity by connecting girls with mentors and educational opportunities, fostering a sense of community and collaboration beyond geographical boundaries.

NGI's strengths include a belief in the power of girls, strong community partnerships, and a dedicated team of volunteers committed to promoting their vision.

NGI envisions a future where girls and women in the Northern Region have access to resources, networking opportunities, and mentorship. We aspire to create a platform for girls to become future mentors themselves.

NGI offers girl-child education support services, including mentorship programs, workshops, and leadership initiatives to enhance the educational experience for girls.

NGI's mission is to connect Northern girls with mentors and various educational and career opportunities, empowering them to become catalysts for positive change within their communities.

NGI was founded by two sisters, Sahadatu and Dr. Namawu Alolo, with the vision of empowering women and girls in the Northern Region.

NGI is a non-governmental organization focused on empowering girls and women from the Northern Region of Ghana through mentorship, education, and career support.

NGI directly tackles obstacles like limited educational access/completion, lack of employment pathways, missing female mentorship/role models, restrictive cultural norms, inadequate skills training, and more.

NGI connects girls with various educational opportunities, including workshops, leadership programs, and mentorship initiatives aimed at enhancing their overall educational experience.

NGI provides career support services, including coaching, job search assistance, workshops, and conferences, to enhance the technical capacities of girls and women in various areas.

NGI addresses socio-cultural barriers through targeted mentorship programs and support services that aim to empower girls to overcome challenges in their educational and career paths.

To become a volunteer, visit the Contact page on NGI's website and express your interest in making a difference.

We serve adolescent girls and young women based in rural Northern Region communities, focused on districts with highest gender inequality and barriers to female advancement.

Key NGI programs include our mentorship pairings, STEM camps, academic scholarships, career readiness workshops, community advocacy training and educational sponsorship initiatives..
